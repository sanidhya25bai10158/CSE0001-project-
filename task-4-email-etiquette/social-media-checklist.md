# Social Media Checklist for College Students  


## Do’s  

**Be mindful of what you comment**  
It’s easy to drop a quick reply and move on, but comments stick around. Make sure what you say adds value instead of creating unnecessary drama.

**Keep your profiles somewhat consistent**  
If someone looks you up (like a recruiter or senior), your profiles shouldn’t give completely different impressions. Try to keep a basic level of consistancy.

**Use social media to learn, not just scroll**  
Follow pages that actually teach you something—whether it’s skills, career advice, or new ideas. It makes your time online feel less wasted.

**Set boundaries for your screen time**  
It’s easy to lose track of hours. Decide when to log off, especially during exams or important work, even if it feels a bit inconvinient.

**Think about your digital footprint long-term**  
What you post now might still be around years later. Try to build an online presence you won’t regret later.

---

## Don’ts  

**Don’t share everything instantly**  
Not every moment needs to be posted right away. Sometimes it’s better to just enjoy things without documenting them.

**Don’t blindly follow trends**  
Just because something is trending doesn’t mean it’s worth doing. Some trends can be risky or just plain embarrassing later.

**Don’t ignore how your tone comes across**  
Text can be misunderstood easily. What feels like a joke to you might come off as rude or harsh to someone else.

**Don’t rely on social media for validation**  
Likes and comments don’t define your worth. It’s easy to get caught up in numbers, but they don’t really mean much in real life.

**Don’t mix every part of your life online**  
It’s okay to keep some things private. You don’t need to share every opinion, plan, or personal detail with everyone.
