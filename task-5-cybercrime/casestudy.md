# Case Study: Identity Theft  

Identity theft is a cybercrime where someone steals another person's personal information and uses it for financial gain or fraudulent activities. This can include details like Aadhar numbers, phone numbers, bank information, or login credentials.

Consider a realistic scenario involving a college student, Ananya. She receives an email that looks like it’s from her bank, asking her to “verify” her account due to suspicious activity. The email looks conivincing, so she clicks the link and enters her details on a fake website. Within a few days, she notices unknown transactions and even finds that a new loan application has been made in her name.

Typically, identity theft follows a pattern. First, the attacker gathers information through phishing emails, fake websites, or data leaks. Next, they use this data to access bank accounts, create new accounts, or impersonate the victim. Finally, they carry out fraudulent transactions before the victim even realizes what’s happening.

This crime often targets students, elderly people, and those who are less cautious online. Many victims don’t notice the signs until it’s too late, which makes the impact worse.

The consequences can be serious—financial loss, damage to credit history, and a long, frustarting process of proving your identity again. It also creates stress and a lasting fear of online transactions.

This case shows how a small moment of trust can lead to big problems, making awareness and caution extremely important.
